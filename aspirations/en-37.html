    That's a good saying! The nearest I can think of would be "to be a money-pit" and it is usually used of things rather than people.

    e.g. John was building his own aeroplane, so far it had cost him $200,000 and the project was only half-finished. He could have bought a new and better aeroplane for $300,000 - the project was a money pit.

    Of people, there is the phrase, "to be high-maintenance" e.g. John regretted giving Jane the use of his credit card. She had turned out to be a high-maintenance girlfriend; she spent over $1,000 a week on clothes and would eat at only the most expensive restaurants, where she insisted on arriving by limousine.
