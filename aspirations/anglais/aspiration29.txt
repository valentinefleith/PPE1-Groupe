        <h1 class="page-header">
<span>Money Services Business Definition</span>
</h1>

<div class="1 bs-2col node node--type-site-page node--view-mode-full">
  

  
    <div>
    
            <div class="field field--name-body field--type-text-with-summary field--label-hidden field--item"><p>The term "money services business" includes any <a href="/person">person</a> doing business, whether or not on a regular basis or as an organized business concern, in one or more of the following capacities:</p>

<p>(1) Currency dealer or exchanger. <br />
(2) Check casher. <br />
(3) Issuer of traveler's checks, money orders or stored value. <br />
(4) Seller or redeemer of traveler's checks, money orders or stored value. <br />
(5) Money transmitter.<br />
(6) U.S. Postal Service.</p>

<p>An activity threshold of greater than $1,000 per person per day in one or more transactions applies to the definitions of: currency dealer or exchanger; check casher; issuer of traveler's checks, money orders or stored value; and seller or redeemer of travelers' checks, money orders or stored value. The threshold applies separately to each activity -- if the threshold is not met for the specific activity, the <a href="/person">person</a> engaged in that activity is not an MSB on the basis of that activity.</p>

<p>No activity threshold applies to the definition of money transmitter. Thus, a person who engages as a business in the transfer of funds is an MSB as a money transmitter, regardless of the amount of money transmission activity.</p>

<p>Notwithstanding the previous discussion, the term "money services business" does not include:</p>

<ul><li>A bank, as that term is defined in 31 CFR 1010.100(d) (formerly 31 CFR 103.11(c)), or</li>
	<li>A person registered with, and regulated or examined by, the Securities and Exchange Commission or the Commodity Futures Trading Commission.</li>
</ul><p>For the complete regulatory definition of "money services business", see 31 CFR 1010.100(ff) (formerly 31 CFR 103.11(uu)).</p>

<p>Note: Each money services business (MSB) is a <a href="/financial-institution-definition">financial institution</a>. For the regulatory definition of "financial institution," see 31 CFR 1010.100(t) (formerly 31 CFR 103.11(n)).</p></div>
